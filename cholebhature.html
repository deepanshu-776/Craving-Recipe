Chole bhature is a popular North Indian dish consisting of spicy chickpea curry (chole) and deep-fried leavened bread (bhature). Here is a recipe for both components, focusing on authentic flavor and the perfect texture.


Part 1: Chole (Spicy Chickpea Curry)
This recipe uses whole spices and a unique technique to get a rich, dark color and complex flavor.


Ingredients:
1 cup dried chickpeas (chole), soaked overnight
1 large onion, finely chopped
2 medium tomatoes, pureed
1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
1 green chili, slit
For the Spice Pouch (Potli Masala):
1 black cardamom
2 cloves
2 green cardamoms
1 cinnamon stick (1 inch)
2 bay leaves
1 tsp tea leaves (for color)
1/2 tsp salt
Dry Spices:
1 tsp coriander powder
1/2 tsp cumin powder
1 tsp chole masala (or garam masala)
1/4 tsp turmeric powder
1/2 tsp red chili powder
Salt to taste
2 tbsp oil or ghee
Fresh coriander for garnish
1 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves), crushed



Instructions:

1.Boil the Chickpeas: Drain the soaked chickpeas. In a pressure cooker, add the chickpeas, enough water to cover them, and the spice pouch (wrapped in a small piece of muslin cloth). Cook for 5-6 whistles or until the chickpeas are very soft and mushy. Discard the spice pouch.


2.Prepare the Gravy: Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until they are golden brown. Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chili, and cook for a minute until the raw aroma disappears.


3.Cook the Tomatoes: Add the tomato puree and all the dry spices (coriander, cumin, chole masala, turmeric, red chili powder). Cook the mixture on low heat until the oil separates from the masala.


4.Combine and Simmer: Add the boiled chickpeas to the pan, along with some of the cooking water from the pressure cooker to adjust the consistency. Add salt and mix well. Bring to a boil, then simmer on low heat for 10-15 minutes, mashing a few chickpeas to thicken the gravy.


5.Finish the Chole: Crush the kasuri methi between your palms and add it to the chole. Garnish with fresh coriander.



Part 2:Bhature (Leavened Fried Bread)
The secret to soft, fluffy bhature is in the dough's fermentation.



Ingredients:
2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
1/2 cup semolina (sooji), fine
1/2 tsp baking powder
1/4 tsp baking soda
1 tsp sugar
1/2 tsp salt
1/4 cup plain yogurt (dahi)
1 tbsp oil
Warm water, as needed for kneading
Oil for deep frying


Instructions: 1.Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the flour, semolina, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and sugar.


2.Make the Dough: Add the yogurt and oil to the flour mixture. Mix everything well. Gradually add warm water and knead the mixture into a soft, smooth, and slightly sticky dough. Knead for at least 10-15 minutes.


3.Ferment the Dough: Cover the dough with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and set it aside in a warm place for at least 2-3 hours. For the best results, let it ferment for 6-8 hours or overnight. The dough should double in size.


4.Prepare for Frying: Punch down the dough to release the air. Divide it into small balls. Gently flatten each ball and roll it into an oval or circular shape (about 4-5 inches in diameter).


5.Fry the Bhature: Heat oil for deep-frying in a kadhai or wok. The oil should be very hot. Gently slide a rolled bhatura into the hot oil. Using a slotted spoon, lightly press down on the center of the bhatura to help it puff up. Flip it and fry the other side until it's golden brown.


6.Serve: Remove the bhatura from the oil and place it on a paper towel to drain excess oil. Serve immediately with the hot chole.
